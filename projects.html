DataCo Smart Supply Chain Analysis
[Excel Dashboard]


View Project View Article

Why: In today's competitive business landscape, understanding and optimizing supply chain operations is crucial for maintaining a competitive edge. The DataCo Global Supply Chain dataset provided a rich opportunity to extract valuable insights that could drive strategic decision-making and operational improvements.

Business Goal: The primary objective of this project was to provide actionable insights that could enhance supply chain efficiency and drive business growth. By visualizing complex data in an accessible format, the dashboard enables stakeholders to:
• Identify high-performing and underperforming segments across various business dimensions.
• Recognize potential risks in the supply chain, particularly regarding late deliveries.
• Make data-driven decisions to optimize inventory management, improve customer satisfaction, and increase overall operational efficiency.

What: I developed an interactive Excel dashboard that offers a comprehensive analysis of DataCo Global's supply chain operations. The dashboard covers multiple facets of the business, including:
• Customer segment analysis
• Market reach
• Product category performance
• Payment method preferences
• Shipping analysis
• Late delivery risk assessment

Key Insights:
• Late Delivery Risks: Late deliveries account for 82% of shipments, with Second Class shipping and regions like Central America and Western Europe facing the highest delays. This impacts customer satisfaction and revenue. Optimizing shipping methods and building regional depots can mitigate these risks.
• Market and Product Insights: The European market has the highest earnings per order, while LATAM leads in order quantity. Premium products like "Computers" drive higher average prices, whereas categories like "Fishing" dominate sales volume. Tailored strategies for these markets and categories can maximize profitability.
• Payment and Fraud Concerns: Debit is the most preferred payment method, but transactions via transfer are linked to fraud. Additionally, debit transactions correlate with late deliveries, posing financial and operational risks. Strengthening payment security and addressing shipping inefficiencies is essential.

Takeaway: This project demonstrates my ability to transform raw data into strategic insights, showcasing my skills in data analysis, visualization, and supply chain management.
