Living the Dream

Hello everyone! I'm Pallavi. A true November Scorpio, born and raised in Mumbai. Back in June 2023, moving to the USA wasn't even part of the plan. Fast forward to June 2024, and I found myself in Seattle, starting my master's degree at the University of Washington. Life really does move fast when it wants to.

What led me here, you might wonder? A growing fascination with supply chain, sparked during my time at Accenture as an SAP Integration Consultant. Add to that a genuine love for learning. I spent over three years studying German for a client engagement and proudly made it to B1 level. Challenging? Absolutely. Worth it? Completely.

Documenting my journey along the way has become one of my favourite pastimes. Capturing moments and turning them into little videos is my creative outlet. I still see myself as a learner, but every attempt makes me better.

Living in Seattle came with many firsts. My first international flight, my first time living alone, and learning what independence truly looks like in a new country. Each experience added a new layer of confidence, perspective, and self-belief.

I'm also a huge K-pop, K-drama, and anime fan. Seventeen and BTS have a special place in my heart. Their passion for music and art pushes me to do my utmost best, and their songs somehow show up exactly when I need comfort. I finally attended my first ever K-pop concert, and yes, it was SEVENTEEN. Those two days were easily some of the best days of my life. I was in awe, emotional, and completely speechless by the end. The funniest part is day one was an impromptu decision made just an hour before the concert, and I somehow ended up with tier one tickets closer to the stage. Still feels unreal.

As I mentioned before, this year came with a lot of firsts. One of my favourite highlights from the past few months was taking a solo trip to San Franscisco. It was equal parts exciting and humbling, the kind of experience that pushes you slightly out of your comfort zone in the best way possible. I spent my days exploring the city, getting lost on purpose, taking in the views, and letting curiosity lead the way. And yes, I did see the Golden Gate Bridge without Carl the fog making an appearance, which felt like the universe being extra kind that day.

I also got a chance to be a barista, something I never expected to enjoy this much. Making coffee turned out to be oddly calming and surprisingly fun. I still dream of having my own little coffee corner at home someday. Here's my first ever iced caramel macchiato that I made, a proud moment.

And somewhere along the way, I entered my obsessed-with-boba phase. Earl Grey, Jasmine Milk Tea and Classic Black tea are some of my Go-Tos. If you ever get the chance, try rose milk with a bit of taro milk tea with boba and mango jelly. Please do. Absolute banger.

On a more serious note, I'm a big believer that what's meant for you will find you. Keep putting in effort, keep showing up, and keep going head fast towards your goals. Things have a way of falling into place when you don't give up. “Believe in yourself” honestly works like magic.

Thank you for scrolling till the end. I'm cheering for you, and I hope you achieve every dream you're working towards. Fly high!
